The development of AI technology in the aerospace field focuses on contributing to the advancement of aerospace science. This research includes the analysis of remote sensing imagery, building extraction from aerial images, and the interpretation of ground observation data, developing advanced AI algorithms for aerospace exploration and Earth observation. This enables more efficient data analysis and interpretation, opening new horizons for aerospace research.

<!--more-->

MacsLAB's research in the aerospace sector aims to improve the efficiency and safety of space exploration using AI technology. The laboratory carries out various projects, such as the automatic processing of satellite imagery, analysis of space mission data, and optimization of air traffic management systems. These studies serve as critical decision-support tools during the design and execution phases of space exploration missions and increase the accuracy and usability of Earth observation data. Additionally, the application of AI technology contributes to cost reduction and increased operational efficiency in the aerospace industry. MacsLAB's research leads technological innovation in the aerospace field and opens new horizons for space exploration.

The research direction of aerospace at MacsLAB lies in the development of AI technologies that contribute to enhancing the efficiency and safety of space exploration. For example, AI systems are being developed to derive optimal response strategies by simulating various scenarios that may occur during space missions. This significantly contributes to increasing the success rate of space exploration missions and ensuring the safety of astronauts. Furthermore, developing AI models that perform monitoring and analysis of climate change and natural disasters using satellite data in Earth orbit is also a major research topic. Such research plays an important role not only in the advancement of aerospace technology but also in global environmental protection and management.

**Key Technologies Utilized**

- Remote Sensing Image Analysis
- Predictive Maintenance
- Space Data Analysis
- Object Detection and Classification
- Simulation and Modeling
- Climate Modeling and Analysis
