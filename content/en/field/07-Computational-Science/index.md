Computational science research at MacsLAB focuses on numerically modeling complex natural and engineering systems and combining them with data-driven methods to provide predictive and explainable interpretations. We expand problems that are difficult to address through experiments alone into simulations and support practical decision-making by increasing consistency with real-world data.

<!--more-->

The laboratory focuses on a hybrid approach that utilizes physics-based models alongside statistical estimation, optimization algorithms, and AI. This enables us to find stable solutions even in high-dimensional parameter spaces and allows for highly reliable predictions even in environments with limited observation data. Furthermore, to systematically manage the trade-off between computational cost and accuracy, we conduct research on model reduction, efficient sampling, and parallel computing strategies.

The direction of computational science research at MacsLAB goes beyond simple performance improvement to securing reproducibility, interpretability, and scalability. For instance, in interpreting medical data, we conduct research to clearly present diagnostic evidence by combining mathematical models reflecting biological mechanisms with deep learning. In aerospace and remote sensing problems, we increase the generalization performance and reliability of models through uncertainty quantification (UQ) and statistical simulation. These approaches serve as a core foundation for enhancing the quality of computation-based decision-making across various domains.

**Key Technologies Utilized**

- Numerical Analysis
- Computational Modeling & Simulation
- Uncertainty Quantification (UQ)
- Inverse Problems
- Stochastic Differential Equations & Statistical Modeling
- High-Performance Computing
- Model Reduction
- Hybrid AI-Physics Models
